Testing of an Intelligent Programming Interface

This job is part of a research study being conducted by Graham Neubig at Carnegie Mellon University. This job involves testing out an intelligent programming interface that allows you to command your computer in English and generate code corresponding to what you want the computer to do. For example, you would enter the command:

* Command: Sort list my_list in reverse alphabetical order

and the computer would generate the below snippet:

* Snippet: my_list.sort(reverse=True, key=lambda x: str(x))

Our hope is that this interface will eventually be useful to programmers, and your feedback on our early prototype will be invaluable in assessing its usefulness.

If you participate in the study, you will be asked to solve several simple programming tasks with and without the interface. We will measure the time required to perform the task, and assess whether the submitted code successfully accomplishes the task or not. We expect that the majority of submitted assignments will be complete and accomplish the stated goals, but 100% task success is not a condition for participation or payment.

In the end, after solving several problems, we will ask you about your impressions of the software and any comments you have.

Qualifications: You must be proficient in Python. Answers will be monitored, and must be reasonably accurate (i.e. obvious examples should almost all be correct) to receive compensation. You must be at least 18 to participate in the study.

Time Involved and Compensation: You will be asked to do 8-10 programming assignments. Based on our experience we estimate that this will take about 30 minutes per assignment (some taking more than others). We will compensate $5 per assignment (no compensation will be given unless all assignments are completed). Once starting the job, you will be expected to complete all assignments within 7 calendar days, and no compensation will be given for late submissions.

Data Usage: In your application to perform the job, we will ask for your contact information and physical location (which we need to know to set up a low-latency server near your location), as well as a number of questions to gauge your Python programming experience. At no time will we share your contact information or location with people outside of our research team.

How to Apply and Right to Withdraw: If you are interested in performing the job, please complete this survey. We will perform a simple screening process based on your programming experience. If we determine that you are eligible to participate, we will give you a link to the study. After you accept the job, you may withdraw any time if you feel that you are not able or willing to perform the tasks involved.

If you have any questions, please contact Frank Xu (fangzhex [at] cs.cmu.edu) and Graham Neubig (gneubig [at] cs.cmu.edu).
